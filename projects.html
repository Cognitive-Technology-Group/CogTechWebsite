OpenBCI Development

What if simple brain-computer interfaces became a common tool for interacting with computers?

Brain computer interfaces (BCI) enable communication between the brain and an external device. While BCI’s have been developed in academic laboratories for decades, the OpenBCI will be the first low-cost, open-source neural interface available to the consumer market. We have the unique opportunity to collaborate directly with OpenBCI to develop the software library for the first public release of the OpenBCI board.

Deliverables

  1. Create a modular, scalable, and approachable Python framework to enable the development of applications that build upon OpenBCI technology.
  2. Build drivers for existing BCI software libraries.

Vibratto

The Vibrato Haptic Vest creates an immersive gaming experience by creating precise tactile feedback to the wearer's torso. Vibrato consists of a vest and sleeves with arrays of vibrotactile actuators that provide precisely-timed vibrations. In addition to gaming interfaces, the wearable tactile device could be used for navigation, sports, dance, or physical training.

Deliverables

  1. Hardware (vest and sleeves with arrays of mini smart vibrators)
  2. Cross-platform API that enables easy game integration

Cognitive Technology Forecasting

The Cognitive Technology Forecasting team aims to identify potential long-term outcomes in the field of neural engineering, to inform our priorities in designing current projects. We will create a timeline and wiki of potential projects in the next 3 to 5 years that hacker groups like ourselves are capable of doing. Our current areas of focus are (1) neural engineering, brain-computer interfaces, biohacking (2) theoretical neuroscience, brain emulation, and (3) immersive technology, virtual reality, sensory input/output,and biosensors.
